Understanding Rate of Change

The rate of change measures how quickly one quantity changes relative to another. In calculus, this concept forms the foundation of derivatives.

When we observe a vehicle's movement, its speed represents the rate at which distance changes over time. For example, 60 miles per hour means the distance increases by 60 miles for each hour that passes.

The Derivative Definition

In the visualization, you can adjust the vehicle's speed and observe how the distance changes over different time intervals. When the time interval is small, we're approaching the instantaneous rate of change, which is exactly what a derivative calculates.

Real-World Application

When driving a car, the speedometer shows the rate of change of distance with respect to time. This is an example of a derivative in action.

Similarly, when analyzing how quickly a population grows, the economy expands, or temperature rises, we're calculating rates of change to understand dynamic processes.
